## Why This Week Matters

A large number of Codeforces problems up to around the 1400-1500 rating range are based on:

- Ad hoc thinking
- Constructive ideas
- Greedy observations
- Prefix sums and frequency counting
- Careful implementation

This is one of the biggest differences between LeetCode and Codeforces. In Codeforces, many beginner and intermediate problems are not about knowing a famous algorithm. They are about noticing the right pattern, building the right observation, and implementing it cleanly.

That is exactly what we will start training this week.

## Codeforces Progression

After completing the above problems, start solving 800-rated problems on Codeforces.

Try to solve around 20-30 problems at this level. There is no fixed number, but use this as a rough checkpoint:

- If you can solve around 7 out of 10 problems on your own, move to 900-rated problems.
- Once 900 starts feeling comfortable, gradually move to higher ratings.
- Do not rush the rating jump. The goal is to build consistency, not just collect solved problems.

## How To Practice

There are two good ways to continue:

1. Use the [Codeforces Problemset](https://codeforces.com/problemset) apply a rating filter, and solve problems randomly.
2. Follow the [TLE Eliminators CP Sheet](https://www.tle-eliminators.com/cp-sheet) which has 31 handpicked problems from each rating range.

I personally suggest the first approach , apply the tag/rating filter on Codeforces and solve random problems. This helps you learn how to face unfamiliar statements, which is very important for contests.

The sheet is also good if you prefer a more structured path.

## Closing Note

Some of you may initially feel that this is hard, and that is completely normal.

Competitive programming becomes easier only after you struggle with enough problems. If a problem does not click immediately, spend time thinking, try small examples, look for patterns, and then read the editorial only after giving it a serious attempt.

Keep going slowly and consistently. The goal this week is not perfection. The goal is to start thinking like a problem solver.

Feel free to reach out if you get stuck.
